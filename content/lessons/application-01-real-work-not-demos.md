## Why this matters

There's a hidden trap with "learn AI" content: most of it has you do toy tasks ("write a poem about a robot", "summarise this Wikipedia article"). You'll get good at toy tasks. Toy tasks won't change your life. The skill that actually compounds is using AI on real work where the stakes — even small ones — make you pay attention.

## The shape of real work

Real work has these properties that toy work doesn't:

- **Someone cares about the output.** A client, your manager, your audience, future-you.
- **There's a "good" you'd recognise.** You know what shipping it would feel like, even if you can't fully describe it.
- **The cost of bad output is non-zero.** A confused email, a missed deadline, a tweet that misfires.
- **You'll learn from the result.** The work feeds back into your skill.

If a task has all four, AI-assisting it teaches you more in 20 minutes than 4 hours of "explore this prompt template" tutorials.

## How to find your first 5 real-work tasks

Open a notes app right now and brainstorm tasks across these buckets:

1. **Communication** — emails, Slack messages, status updates, meeting notes you usually skip writing.
2. **Drafting** — proposals, briefs, scripts, documentation.
3. **Reviewing** — feedback you'd give but don't have time to write up.
4. **Thinking** — decisions you've been postponing because you don't have time to lay them out.
5. **Repetitive** — tasks you do every week that could be 70% AI-drafted.

Pick the ONE that, if you AI-assisted it this week, would give back 30 minutes. Start there.

## What you'll notice

The first time you AI-assist real work end-to-end:

- You'll be a worse editor than you thought. (Most of the bad output is hard to spot.)
- You'll be a faster producer than you thought. (Drafting time collapses.)
- You'll have feelings about it. (Sometimes "this is great." Sometimes "I feel weird about this." Both are useful signals.)

## What to avoid

- **Doing toy tasks for "practice."** Practice on toy = good at toys. Practice on real = good at real.
- **Saving the "important" work for when you're "ready."** You're never going to feel ready. Use it on something small but real today.
- **Comparing yourself to internet demos.** They're cherry-picked. Your messy real-work output is more honest data.

## Try it

Open a Project here in this app — or make one. In the next 30 minutes, AI-draft one piece of real work using the coach: an email you owe someone, a draft tweet, a meeting note, a scoping doc. Edit it. Ship it. Note what was useful and what was off. That's the loop.
