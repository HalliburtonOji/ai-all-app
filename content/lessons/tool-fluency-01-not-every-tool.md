## Why this matters

You can lose entire weeks evaluating tools instead of using them. The marketing for new AI tools is relentless and intentionally FOMO-shaped. A small, deliberate toolkit you actually keep open is worth more than a tab graveyard.

## The honest count

Most professionals doing real AI-assisted work use **three to five tools**. Not fifty. The shape is usually:

1. **A general-purpose chat model.** Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini — pick one, get good at it. Switching constantly resets your fluency.
2. **One image tool, if you make visual stuff.** Replicate / Midjourney / a built-in app feature. One.
3. **A workspace where the work lives.** Notion, Obsidian, your codebase, your CRM — wherever the actual work happens.
4. **A capture tool.** Voice memos, a notes app, a single inbox. AI works on what you give it; the bottleneck is usually input, not the model.

Optional, only if you genuinely need it: a TTS voice tool (ElevenLabs), a video editor, a code-specific assistant.

## Signals it's worth adding a tool

- It removes a real bottleneck you're hitting weekly. Not theoretically.
- You can afford it without flinching at the bill.
- You'd use it three or more times a week. Not "saves the option."
- The thing it does isn't well-handled by your existing chat model.

## Signals to skip

- You saw a viral demo. Demos lie. Wait two weeks; if it still looks great, look again.
- Someone says "this changes everything."
- You can't articulate the specific job-to-be-done.
- The pricing has weird "save 80% by paying yearly" pressure copy.

## Sticky over shiny

The tools you keep using have one thing in common: they fit invisibly into your workflow. They don't ask for ceremony, don't require a fresh "AI-first mindset," don't have a learning curve longer than the value they unlock.

When something asks more of you than it gives back, drop it. There's always another tool tomorrow.

## Try it

Audit your last 30 days. Which tools did you use 5+ times? Which did you sign up for and barely open? Cancel one subscription this week. Notice what doesn't break.
