## Where AI is genuinely useful in this job

- **First-draft visuals.** Mood boards, low-fidelity comps, illustration starters, hero images for landing pages, social tiles. Use AI to compress the empty-canvas problem.
- **Brand voice extension.** When a brand voice is locked in, AI can extend it into copy variants — but you're still the editor.
- **Bulk variations.** Resize, recolor, extend, swap backgrounds. The boring middle of design execution.
- **Brief-to-pitch translation.** Turning a half-formed client brief into a clear "what we're building" doc you can both agree on.
- **Critique on demand.** Paste a mockup + ask the model to critique it as a [specific persona] — useful for stress-testing before client review.

## Where AI is not the right tool

- **The taste call.** Choosing between three valid directions is judgment, not pattern-match. The model will pick the safe-average one, which is often the wrong call.
- **Brand strategy from zero.** "What should this company stand for?" — not until you have inputs from real people.
- **Custom illustration with a consistent character.** Models drift across renders. For comics, mascots, recurring characters, you'll burn more time fixing inconsistencies than just drawing it.
- **Final polish.** The last 10% (kerning, alignment, micro-interactions, the things clients can't articulate but feel) is yours.

## Three indispensable moves

1. **Build a personal style library.** Save the references, the typefaces, the palettes, the "this was the brief" pairings that worked. AI tools amplify what you give them — your library is the leverage.
2. **Be the explainer.** Clients pay you for the *case* — why this works, why the alternatives don't. Practice talking through choices in 60-second voice notes you record before client meetings. AI can draft your copy but it can't be the human who made the call.
3. **Get fast at evaluation.** When AI gives you 8 variants, the bottleneck moves from "make one" to "pick the best one and say why." Treat evaluation as a craft. Time yourself.

## Smart defaults

- Use the Studio image tool here for first-draft moodboards. Refine in Figma / your real tool — don't fight the AI tile.
- Pricing pressure: AI lowers the floor (cheap clients now have alternatives) but raises the ceiling (good designers ship more, faster). Stay near the ceiling — your portfolio + taste is the moat.
- Disclose-AI when relevant. Clients who care will care; clients who don't, don't.

## What to avoid

- Selling "AI design services" as a category. You're a designer; AI is a tool. Don't shrink yourself to "the AI prompt person."
- Burning hours on tools that promise to replace your decision-making. They don't, and the time you spent learning them is gone.
