## Where AI is genuinely useful in this job

- **Variant generation.** Headlines, subject lines, ad copy, CTAs, landing-page hooks. The hard part is testing them; the easy part is producing them. AI compressed the easy part to seconds.
- **Audience research compression.** Paste a long competitor analysis or interview transcript and ask for "the 3 jobs-to-be-done patterns I'm hearing." Useful when you have raw inputs and not enough hours.
- **Brief drafting.** Turning a stakeholder's vague ask into a structured brief everyone can review.
- **Onboarding sequences and lifecycle email.** First-draft of full multi-touch flows in minutes.
- **Reporting narrative.** Numbers + "what changed" — AI can draft the "so what" paragraph, you sharpen it.

## Where AI is not the right tool

- **The actual customer conversation.** Interviewing, observing, listening. AI can summarise the recording. It can't be in the room.
- **Brand judgment under pressure.** "Should we run this campaign during the news cycle?" — that's a human-context call.
- **Original positioning.** AI averages what already exists. New category creation, repositioning, pricing-as-message — all judgment calls grounded in real-world signal AI doesn't have.
- **Numbers you'll cite externally.** Don't trust an AI-generated "industry benchmark" stat. Verify or remove.

## Three indispensable moves

1. **Own your data.** When AI commodifies copy, the moat moves to information advantage. First-party data, customer interviews, survey results, sales call notes. Pros who have data their competitors don't are ahead.
2. **Get sharper on positioning, not louder on output.** Volume is now cheap. The thing AI can't replicate is "this brand stands for something specific." Spend your hours on the strategy doc, not the 17th tweet variant.
3. **Develop a measurement habit.** AI lets you run more experiments faster. The bottleneck moves to deciding which experiments matter and reading the results honestly. Most marketers are still bad at this.

## Smart defaults

- Studio text drafter is for first-draft email and ad copy.
- Image tool is for hero/social tiles when you need a placeholder before final design.
- Coach is for thinking out loud about positioning and audience — way more useful than asking it to "write the campaign."

## What to avoid

- Stuffing the funnel with AI-generated low-quality content. Search engines and humans both penalise this now.
- "AI-powered" as your selling point. Customers don't care; outcomes do.
- Outsourcing taste. Final word on tone, brand fit, and timing is always yours.
